Bowling Game

Overview

I loved going bowling growing up, but was always confused as to how the scoring was calculated. This gave me the idea to create a bowling score calculator.

Instructions

Normal game play

The calculator consists of 10 frames, where the player selects how many pins are knocked down.
The current frame for the player is highlighted

Within each frame, the player has 2 opportunities to knock all ten pins down.
If less than 10 pins are knocked down, the total number of pins knocked down is displayed in the frame. Scores will be tallied into the frame in question.
As per below example, a total of 6 was scored in frame 1 and 3 was scored in frame 2 hence a total of 9 is displayed.

Strikes

Knock all 10 pins down on your first throw - This is called a strike and indicated by an X. 10 points are added to your score as well as the sum of the next 2 throws.
As per below example, the player got a strike on his first throw (10 points) plus 8 for the next 2 throws, hence a total of 18 was scored for frame one.

Spares

Knock all 10 pins in 2 throws - This is called a spare and indicated by a slash. 10 points are added to your score as well as the sum of the next throw only
As per below example, the player got a 6 on his first throw, 4 on his second throw and scored a spare. (10 points) plus 5 for the next 1 throw only hence a total of 15 was scored for frame one.
